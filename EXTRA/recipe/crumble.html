Apple and Blackberry Crumble

Four big cooking apples.
A litre of frozen, rinsed blackberries.
200 grams of white flour.
75-100 grams of butter.
100 grams of sugar.

Hint:
Basically it's almost 2:1:1 flour:butter:sugar.

Serves six.

Hat tip to Bex.

- Preheat the oven to 180 degrees Celcius.

- Peel, core, and chop the apples.
- Throw the apples into a saucepan with a few tablespoons of water and a spoon or two of sugar.
- Heat and stir the apples until they're nice and soft, but a few big lumps remaining is OK.

- Chuck the flour and butter in a bowl and knead and massage it all together until it's in little and big crumbles.
- Add the sugar and mix it all up good.

- Pour the apple sauce into a shallow baking dish, put the blackberries on top, then top it off with the crumble mixture.
- Make sure to cover all the apple and blackberry sauce mix.
- Pop it in the oven for 30-45 minutes.

- Suggestion: You'll know it's done when it looks light-brown and some fruit starts to bubble up around the edges. Don't let it go black, but the flour must be cooked.
