Turkish Eggs

Two cups of plain yogurt.
Two eggs.
One clove of garlic.
A lash of vinegar.
One tablespoon of salt.

Garnish:
Four tablespoons of butter.
Two tablespoons of red or cayenne pepper.
Mint.
Coriander.

Serves two.

- In a large pot, boil water with a pinch of salt and the vinegar.

- Put the yogurt in a bowl, crush in the garlic, add the salt.
- Whisk the yogurt, garlic, and salt together.
- Ladle the mixture onto two plates.

- When the water is boiled, turn the heat down to simmer.
- Stir the water vigorously to get the vortex going. Fun!
- Crack the eggs into the centre of the pot, side by side.
- Let the eggs cook for about three minutes, depending on how you like them poached.
- Take the eggs out with a slotted spoon.
- Place the eggs over the yogurt on the plates.

- Melt the butter in a small skillet and add the red pepper, mint, and coriander.
- Pour the garnish over the eggs when it starts to bubble.

- Suggestion: Serve this dish while still hot with bread on the side.
