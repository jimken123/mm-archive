Hall of Fame

Old Reports







Diego Maradona gets his Hall of Fame award for single-handedly winning the World Cup in 1986. You have to admire a man who shoots paparazzi with an air rifle, snorts mountains of cocaine, and is still a God to the people of Argentina and Naples.

George Best receives his award for being the greatest Irish footballer of all time. For dedication to the champagne life, FA Cup goals, and Miss Worlds. We also love the Wedding Present album named after him.

Eric Cantona is in our Hall of Fame for bringing that Gallic cool to the game. Check out the quizzically raised eyebrow. For wearing his collar up, Kung Fu, and poetry - he is like a seagull following a trawler, apparently.

 

Pele is inducted into the Hall of Fame for still being in a class of his own, despite having retired before any of us were even born. The original Brazilian. 

Costa Rican Kevin Keegan get the award for the fastest feet in the game - scroll over our club crest at the top of the page to see the evidence. Also for reviving a haircut not seen since Kevin Keegan left Hamburg.

Zinedane Zidane receives an award for those moments of brilliance he produces every now and again, just when you think he's drifted out of the game.

 

Ronaldo gets his award for the contributions to football he has yet to make. Get a move on you lazy git, you're too old to be the wunderkid any more. So far, we are unconvinced and await the proof of the potential.

KP gets his Hall of Fame award for unwavering commitment to Herbert Park Rangers F.C. and to every tackle he makes on the other 21 players on the pitch. Dangerous to play against; dangerous to play with.
